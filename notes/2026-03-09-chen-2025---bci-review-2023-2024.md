## Summary

Comprehensive review of brain-computer interface advances during the 2023-2024 period, covering both invasive and non-invasive techniques from fundamental mechanisms to clinical applications. The review documents a period of rapid progress in which BCI hardware and software evolved substantially, with innovative BCI inventions increasingly being introduced into clinical practice. The authors argue that both invasive and non-invasive BCI technologies are paving the way for broader clinical applications that extend from disease treatment to human function enhancement.

## Key Findings

- **2023-2024 saw numerous research breakthroughs and clinical applications** — the field is accelerating, not just incrementally progressing
- **Both invasive and non-invasive BCIs** are moving toward broader clinical deployment, not just lab demonstrations
- **BCI hardware and software continue to evolve** in tandem with deepening understanding of basic medical principles
- **Clinical applications expanding** from the traditional focus on motor control and communication toward a wider range of therapeutic and assistive uses
- The field is transitioning from **laboratory demonstration to clinical practice** — a qualitative shift in maturity
- BCIs are expected to offer **disease treatment, enhancement of human bodily functions, and improved quality of life** — the authors explicitly include enhancement alongside therapeutic applications

## Methodology

Narrative review covering the 2023-2024 period in medical BCI development. Organized by technology type (invasive vs. non-invasive) and spanning from fundamental neurobiological mechanisms to real-world clinical implementations. Published in Brain-X (Wiley), a journal focused on brain science and technology.

## Relevance to This Work

**Directly supports Section IX (Enhancement Beyond Therapy).** The manuscript draft uses Cinel et al. 2019 to anchor the BCI discussion but notes in its gaps table that a "[NEEDED] Recent BCI review would update the Cinel 2019 landscape with more current data on clinical translation." Chen et al. 2025 fills this gap precisely. It confirms and extends the trajectory Cinel predicted — BCIs moving from lab to clinic — but with the added weight of documenting the 2023-2024 acceleration. The finding that BCIs are now entering clinical practice (not just field testing) supports the manuscript's argument that "the field is moving from laboratory demonstration toward a growing set of clinical applications." It also reinforces the urgency argument: if BCI governance frameworks are needed, they are needed now, not when the technology matures further.
