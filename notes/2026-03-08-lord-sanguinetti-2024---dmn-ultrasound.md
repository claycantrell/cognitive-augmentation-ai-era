## Summary

Pilot study from the SEMA Lab. Targeted the posterior cingulate cortex (PCC) — a key hub of the default mode network (DMN) — with transcranial focused ultrasound during fMRI. The DMN is associated with self-referential thinking, mind-wandering, and the "narrative self." Results showed TUS modulated DMN connectivity and participants reported altered subjective experience. Significant because the DMN is exactly what changes in experienced meditators.

## Key Findings

- TUS to the posterior cingulate cortex modulated default mode network connectivity
- Participants reported altered subjective experience during stimulation
- The PCC is a hub of the DMN — the network most associated with mind-wandering, self-referential thought, and rumination
- Reduced DMN activity is one of the most consistent findings in experienced meditators
- This study shows you can modulate the same network with ultrasound instead of 10,000+ hours of meditation
- Published from the SEMA Lab (Sonication Enhanced Mindful Awareness) — explicitly designed to bridge ultrasound and meditation
- Collaboration with X (Google's Moonshot Factory) — signals serious institutional interest
- 20 citations (recent paper, still accumulating)

## Methodology

- Pilot study (small sample)
- fMRI during TUS targeting the posterior cingulate cortex
- Measured changes in DMN functional connectivity
- Collected subjective experience reports from participants
- Double-blind, sham-controlled

## Relevance to This Work

**Strong evidence for both Sections V and VI.** This is the most direct bridge between "meditation as proof of concept" and "enhancement as delivery mechanism." The DMN is the specific brain network that changes with decades of meditation practice (less rumination, more present-moment awareness, greater metacognition). Sanguinetti's lab is showing you can modulate it with a device. For our paper: the target (DMN modulation) is validated by meditation research; the delivery mechanism (TUS) is being demonstrated in the lab. The collaboration with X/Google's Moonshot Factory also signals this isn't fringe — major institutions are investing.
