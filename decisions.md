## Scope & Framing
- **v5 (FINAL): Paper argues for human capacity-building as a neglected AI governance dimension.** Three complementary paths: adult therapeutic intervention, adult enhancement, early educational development. Tone is precise and academic, not prophetic. The paper is a normative-policy argument with a conceptual framework contribution.
- **Three core contributions identified:**
  1. A framework for treating human capacity-building as AI governance
  2. The distinction between capacity-building enhancement and dependency-producing pseudo-enhancement
  3. A layered strategy spanning adult intervention, adult enhancement, and early developmental education
- **Five categories of intervention:** pharmacological, neurotechnological, therapeutic/behavioral, contemplative/reflective, developmental/educational.
- **"Assisted intervention" is the adult-side framing.** Combined models (drugs+therapy, stimulation+therapy) rather than standalone tools. Matches how serious clinical work actually functions.
- **Psychedelic-assisted therapy is the paradigm case** for combined intervention — chemistry + guided support + structured integration.
- **Good vs bad enhancement distinction (Section V) is central.** Capacity-building enhancement vs pseudo-enhancement vs "chemical opium" risk. This is one of the paper's key conceptual contributions.
- **Contemplative practice restored as standalone section (XI).** It serves as proof of concept that relevant capacities are trainable, and as a model for what therapeutic/technological scaffolds might accelerate. Careful not to imply technology replaces wisdom traditions.

## Key Content Decisions
- **Evidence tiering is explicit.** rTMS = FDA-cleared. Psilocybin = promising, no FDA approval. MDMA = FDA rejection August 2024. Near/medium/long-term horizons.
- **Annaka Harris is an entry point, not the anchor.** Her children's mindfulness work illustrates early metacognitive training. Broader ecosystem (CASEL, Harvard, Immordino-Yang) provides scholarly depth.
- **Immordino-Yang bridges education to the core claim.** Civic reasoning and "transcendent thinking" connect directly to AI stewardship capacities.
- **The lifespan framing is key.** Adult therapeutic intervention for near-term. Adult enhancement for medium-term. Educational intervention for long-term population resilience. All three needed.
- **BCI treated as case study in acceleration and risk (IX.C)**, not as a standalone section. Shows how fast neurotech moves from theory to application.
- **Restorative psychopharmacology added as VIII.E subsection.** Depression as the "arrived" case, anger/aggression as mixed, paranoia/psychosis as cautionary. Framed as precision adult intervention within the assisted-intervention lane, not a standalone pillar. The key question is durable agency gains vs short-term symptom relief. Section VIII grows from ~800 to ~1000 words.
