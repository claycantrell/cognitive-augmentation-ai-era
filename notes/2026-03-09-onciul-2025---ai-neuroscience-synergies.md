## Summary

Open-access review examining the bidirectional relationship between artificial intelligence and neuroscience, published in the Journal of Clinical Medicine. The paper documents how AI algorithms — from deep learning to neuromorphic computing — are transforming neuroscience by enabling analysis of complex neural datasets from neuroimaging, electrophysiology, and genomic profiling. Key applications include early detection of neurological disorders, enhanced brain-computer interfaces, and personalized medicine. Crucially, the review also covers the reverse direction: neuroscience is inspiring AI innovations, with biological neural architectures shaping learning algorithms and explainable AI models.

## Key Findings

- **AI is revolutionizing neuroimaging analysis** — deep learning enables processing of complex neural datasets that were previously intractable
- **Bidirectional exchange:** Neuroscience reciprocally influences AI development; biological principles inform improved algorithms and interpretability
- **Key clinical applications:** Early identification of brain disorders, brain-machine interaction systems, and treatment personalization
- **Breakthroughs in dynamic connectivity mapping** — AI enables real-time analysis of how brain regions interact over time
- **Real-time neural decoding** — AI-powered systems can decode neural signals with increasing accuracy and speed
- **Adaptive closed-loop systems** — AI enables BCI systems that respond dynamically to neural states rather than operating on fixed parameters
- **Challenges identified:** Data integration across modalities, ethical considerations around neural data, and AI transparency/explainability remain significant obstacles
- **65 citations** already (published January 2025) — high engagement indicates the topic's timeliness

## Methodology

Narrative review synthesizing recent advances at the intersection of AI and neuroscience. Covers multiple domains including neuroimaging, electrophysiology, genomic profiling, brain-computer interfaces, and drug discovery. Published as open-access (CC-BY) in the Journal of Clinical Medicine. Authors are from Romanian medical and neuroscience institutions.

## Relevance to This Work

**Directly supports Section IX (Enhancement Beyond Therapy).** The manuscript draft argues that "AI is transforming drug discovery by accelerating target identification, compound screening, and aspects of trial design" and that "AI is also being applied to neuroscience analysis, biomarker identification, and aspects of trial design." The Section IX gaps table lists "[NEEDED] AI in neuroscience review — would strengthen the claim that AI is accelerating neuroscience analysis and biomarker identification." Onciul et al. 2025 fills this gap directly. It documents the specific mechanisms by which AI accelerates neuroscience — not just in drug discovery (already covered by Serrano 2024) but in neuroimaging analysis, real-time neural decoding, connectivity mapping, and closed-loop BCI systems. This strengthens the paper's central paradox argument: AI simultaneously threatens human capacity through dependency while accelerating the very tools that might strengthen it.
