## Summary
He et al. conducted the first systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating the efficacy of non-invasive brain stimulation (NIBS) -- primarily tDCS and rTMS -- combined with psychosocial interventions for depression. Analyzing 17 studies with 660 participants, they found that the combination significantly alleviates moderate-to-severe depression and shows a large effect compared to NIBS alone (SMD = -0.84), but does not significantly outperform sham NIBS combined with psychotherapy or psychosocial intervention alone.

## Key Findings
- **Overall effect of combined NIBS + psychosocial intervention on depression:** SMD = -0.36 (95% CI: -0.67, -0.05), I-squared = 68% -- small but significant effect.
- **Moderate-to-severe depression:** SMD = -0.46 (95% CI: -0.90, -0.02), I-squared = 73% -- small-to-moderate effect; combination was effective.
- **Minimal-to-mild depression:** SMD = -0.12 (95% CI: -0.42, 0.18), I-squared = 0%, p = .63 -- not significant; combination did not help mild cases.
- **Combined vs. NIBS alone:** SMD = -0.84 (95% CI: -1.25, -0.42), I-squared = 0% -- large effect favoring combination. This is the strongest finding.
- **Combined vs. sham NIBS + psychosocial:** SMD = -0.12 (95% CI: -0.31, 0.07), I-squared = 0% -- not significant, suggesting the psychosocial component may be driving most of the benefit.
- **Combined vs. psychosocial alone:** SMD = -0.97 (95% CI: -2.32, 0.38), I-squared = 72% -- not significant, though high heterogeneity.
- **Meta-regression:** Stimulation site was significantly associated with effect sizes -- frontal cortex stimulation was superior to central cortex (beta = 0.74, p = .02). Other covariates (age, clinical condition, total sessions, stimulation sequence, tDCS current density) were not significant moderators.
- **Stimulation protocols:** Most tDCS used 2.0 mA current; stimulation time 20-30 min; total sessions ranged 5-15 for tDCS, 10-40 for rTMS.
- **Safety:** Generally well-tolerated; common adverse events were headache, scalp pain, skin redness, itching. One seizure reported after 4 sessions of rTMS.
- **Drop-out rates:** Mean 5.05%, ranging from 0% to 37.84%.
- Placebo effects in NIBS trials are substantial (previous meta-analyses: tDCS sham g = 1.09; rTMS sham g = 0.80), complicating interpretation.

## Methodology
Systematic review and meta-analysis following PRISMA 2020 guidelines. Searched PubMed, Embase, PsycINFO, Web of Science, and Medline from inception to June 2021. Included RCTs and non-RCTs comparing NIBS + psychosocial intervention to control conditions (sham NIBS + psychosocial, NIBS alone, psychosocial alone) in adults with depressive symptoms. 17 studies, 660 participants. Used random-effects model with Hedges' g. Quality assessed with Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0. Meta-regression for covariates. Registered: PROSPERO CRD42021273363.

## Relevance to This Work
This paper is relevant to Section VIII (brain stimulation + therapy). It provides evidence for the synergistic potential of combining neurotechnology (NIBS) with psychological interventions, directly supporting the manuscript's argument that capacity-building works best when biological and psychological modalities are integrated. The finding that NIBS + psychotherapy produces a large effect (d = 0.84) compared to NIBS alone, but no significant advantage over psychotherapy alone, is nuanced -- it suggests the "state-dependent" nature of brain stimulation (stimulation effects depend on concurrent cognitive engagement) and that the psychosocial component provides essential scaffolding. The null finding vs. sham NIBS + psychotherapy highlights the importance of placebo effects and the need for more rigorous research, which should be discussed as a limitation.

## Key Quote
"NIBS combined with psychosocial intervention was effective in alleviating moderate to severe depression but not among individuals with minimally to mildly depressive symptoms... a larger effect size of combined interventions on depression was found when comparing with either NIBS alone [SMD = -0.84, 95% CI (-1.25, -0.42)]."
